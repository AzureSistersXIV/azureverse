About Dethanon Hotgo
Dethanon Hotgo lived his childhood in the Hotgo tribe. Dreamer and fascinated by the stars, he decided to travel beyond the Steppe of Azim. He became mercenary in the Hingashi region, traveling and discovering all kinds of civilizations. Not scared by trials, he tried many meals, many traditions and many fights. His inner beast, raised to be territorial and powerful, gave him the edge against many enemies. His reputation gained levels per the number of fights he won. At least, it permitted him to join a big band of mercenaries.

His time in this band is sketchy. His free time stretched think and sightings of him became rare. His mood became somber and he tended to drink easily. Kugane citizens who had contacts with him lost all links to him. This is after five years he was recovered in a slum at he borders of Kugane. Battered, poorly dressed, with injuries on his torso. It took him several days to recover and when asked why, he replied "I left the group..."

He became a recluse, taking on solo mercenary missions.

This is how he found himself in Kyofu-no-sato. A submarine village, with a big palace where lived the Princess of Azure Currents. His mission was to deliver packages from the surface, rare interaction of the self-sufficient village. His curious side woke up at the sight of the landscape he found there, as well as the people he met. The elderly smiled to him before telling stories of the village, the children asked him to play after getting sweets from him. Young women flirted with him. One amongst the other was attracted to him, as much he was attracted to her. It was when he declared his love for her that he learned that she was the future Princess, Jasmine Istana.

Their love have birth to a happy child and very sociable daughter, Désirée. To the surprise of many in Kyofu-no-sato, he became very thoughtful and more diplomat. He lost his edge. Maybe too much as he met old companions of his mercenary days. Unwilling to let go of his family and eager to protect his child, he fought them. To his death.
